<p><p>The digital thermopile sensor market comprises two primary types: thermopile infrared sensors and thermopile laser sensors. Thermopile infrared sensors detect thermal radiation from objects, converting it into an electrical signal, making them ideal for non-contact temperature measurement in various applications, including HVAC and automotive. In contrast, thermopile laser sensors are designed to measure the intensity of laser beams, ensuring precise readings in industrial and scientific settings. Both types leverage thermoelectric principles, offering advantages in sensitivity and accuracy for temperature and energy detection.</p></p>
