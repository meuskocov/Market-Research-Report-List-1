<p><p>The underground utility locator market comprises various technologies for detecting subsurface utilities. Electromagnetic induction utilizes electromagnetic fields to trace buried lines. Radio detection employs radio waves to identify utilities by measuring signals. Acoustic locating relies on sound waves to pinpoint buried features. Ground-penetrating radar (GPR) uses radar pulses to create subsurface images. Magnetic locators detect disturbances in magnetic fields from ferrous materials. Wires and markers are used for identification and tracing, while "others" includes emerging technologies and methodologies in utility detection.</p></p>
