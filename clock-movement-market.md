<p><p>Clock movement types refer to the mechanisms that drive timekeeping functions. Standard movement utilizes quartz technology for precise timekeeping. Sweep movement features a continuous second hand for smoother motion, minimizing ticking sounds. Pendulum movement is governed by a swinging pendulum, enhancing accuracy traditionally in mechanical clocks. Alarm movement includes a mechanism to produce sound at set times. Other market types encompass specialized movements, such as astronomical clocks or programmable digital displays, catering to unique timekeeping needs.</p></p>
