<p><p>The hyaluronic acid market can be segmented into two primary types: microbial fermentation and animal tissue. Microbial fermentation involves producing hyaluronic acid through the fermentation of naturally occurring bacteria, offering a sustainable and vegan-friendly alternative. This method is often preferred for its consistency and lower risk of contamination. Conversely, the animal tissue market derives hyaluronic acid from animal sources, such as rooster combs or bovine tissues. While this method can yield high purity levels, it raises concerns regarding ethical sourcing and animal welfare.</p></p>
