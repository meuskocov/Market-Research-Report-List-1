<p><p>Non-halogenated flame retardants are chemicals used to reduce the flammability of materials without containing halogens like chlorine or bromine. They are considered more environmentally friendly and safer alternatives to halogenated flame retardants, as they produce less toxic smoke and corrosive gases when exposed to fire. Common types include phosphorus-based, nitrogen-based, and inorganic flame retardants, which are widely used in industries such as construction, electronics, automotive, and textiles.</p><p>The Non-Halogenated Flame Retardants Market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing regulatory restrictions on halogenated compounds and rising demand for sustainable and eco-friendly materials.
