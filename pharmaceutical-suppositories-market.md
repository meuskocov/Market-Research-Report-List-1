<p><p>The pharmaceutical suppositories market is divided into three primary types: rectal, vaginal, and urethral suppositories. Rectal suppositories are designed for administration through the rectum, often used for systemic effects or localized treatment. Vaginal suppositories target the female reproductive tract, providing localized therapy for conditions like infections or hormonal issues. Urethral suppositories, though less common, are used to deliver medications directly into the urethra, typically for conditions like erectile dysfunction or urinary tract issues, allowing for rapid absorption.</p></p>
