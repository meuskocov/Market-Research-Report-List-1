<p><p>The LiDAR distance sensor market is primarily divided into two types: solid-state and mechanical. Solid-state LiDAR sensors feature a compact design with no moving parts, enhancing reliability and durability, making them suitable for various applications, including autonomous vehicles and drones. In contrast, mechanical LiDAR sensors utilize rotating components to capture high-resolution 3D maps, offering superior accuracy and range but often at the expense of size and durability. Both types cater to diverse industry needs, from robotics to environmental monitoring.</p></p>
