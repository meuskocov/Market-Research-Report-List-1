<p><p>PERC (Passivated Emitter and Rear Cell) technology is an advanced solar cell design known for its enhanced efficiency and performance compared to traditional solar cells. By adding a passivation layer at the rear of the cell, PERC cells reduce electron recombination, allowing for increased light absorption and improved energy conversion rates. This technology has gained significant traction in the solar energy market due to the rising demand for renewable energy solutions and the push towards higher efficiency solar panels.</p><p>The PERC Cells Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 9.2% during the forecast period. Latest trends indicate a growing interest in bifacial PERC cells, which capture sunlight on both sides, further enhancing energy generation capabilities. <p><p>PERC cells, or Passivated Emitter and Rear Cells, are advanced solar cells designed for enhanced efficiency. In the market, they are categorized into single-sided and double-sided options. Single-sided PERC cells generate power from one side, utilizing conventional installation methods. In contrast, double-sided PERC cells capture sunlight from both sides, leveraging reflected light from surrounding surfaces, ultimately increasing energy output. This versatility allows users to optimize performance based on installation environments and energy needs, driving demand in the solar industry.</p></p>
