<p><p>High Temperature Superconducting (HTS) fibers are categorized into two main types: 1G HTS and 2G HTS. </p><p>1G HTS refers to the first generation of superconductors, typically made from metal wires with limited flexibility and scalability. In contrast, 2G HTS represents the second generation, featuring coated conductors that provide higher performance, greater flexibility, and improved scalability for various applications. The market for these fibers is driven by their potential in power transmission, medical imaging, and advanced communication systems, benefiting from their low energy loss and enhanced efficiency.</p></p>
