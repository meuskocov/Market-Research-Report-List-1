<p><p>Computer-Aided Drug Discovery (CADD) services encompass various methodologies aimed at streamlining drug development. Virtual screening involves computationally evaluating large compound libraries to identify potential drug candidates. Molecular docking simulates the interaction between molecules to predict binding affinity. Molecular modeling creates three-dimensional representations of molecules to understand their behavior. Scaffold hopping involves modifying existing chemical structures to discover new drug-like candidates. Other services may include quantitative structure-activity relationship (QSAR) modeling and pharmacophore modeling, enhancing the overall efficiency of drug discovery processes.</p></p>
