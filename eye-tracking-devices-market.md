<p><p>The eye tracking devices market features two main types: remote eye trackers and head-mounted eye trackers. Remote eye trackers, typically used in stationary settings, allow users to maintain a natural posture while interacting with screens, capturing eye movements from a distance. In contrast, head-mounted eye trackers are wearable devices that track eye movement in real-time in various environments, offering greater mobility for tasks in virtual reality or dynamic scenarios. Both types play crucial roles in research, usability testing, and game development.</p></p>
