<p><p>The microbial surfactant market encompasses various types, primarily including rhamnolipids and sophorolipids, along with other surfactants. Rhamnolipids, produced by specific bacteria, are known for their effective emulsifying and foaming properties, making them suitable for applications in cosmetics, food, and bioremediation. Sophorolipids, derived from yeast, offer excellent surface-active properties and are widely used in personal care products and detergents. Other microbial surfactants include fatty acid esters and lipopeptides, which further enhance the market's diversity and applicability across multiple industries.</p></p>
